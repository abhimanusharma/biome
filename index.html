Brief Introduction

A biome is a large community of vegetation and wildlife adopted to a specific climate.

There are five major types of biomes -

  1. Aquatic
  2. Grassland

But in this biome project, we are going to focus on tundra.

* Average temperature - -30 to 20 degree fahrenheit

Adaptation for survival

*Climate

Tundra ecosystem are treeless regions found in the arctic where the climate is cold and windy. The rainfall is scant. Tundra lands are mostly covered with snow.

*Seasons

There are two main seasons - Winters and summers in the polar tundra areas. During winters, it is cold and dark. The summers last about 6 to 10 weeks

Adaptation for the survival

A small change in structure is an physical adaptation that helps biome survive.

Plants in the biome

Plants have adopted to the arctic tundra by developing the ability to grow under a layer of snow to carry out photosynthesis in extremely cold temperature. A small leaf structure is another physical adaptation that helps plants survive.

Animals in the biome

Animals such as Reindeer, Polar bear, Muskox have many adaptations to survive in this harsh environment. Animals need shelter and insulation. Animals here tend to have thicks and warmer feathers and fur. Many of them have larger bodies but short arms & legs.

Air quality of tundra

Air pollution effects tundra environment in different ways. Recently arctic clouds are particularly sensitive to air pollution which spurs cloud formation and has a blanketing effect. Black carbon from Diesel engines, fires and other combustion can settle on snow and cause faster melting.

Threats to Tundra

* Climates change - warmer climate creates potential feedback loops that encourage distabilization of tundra ecosystems whereas, higher temperatures drive the growth of shrubs. This changes soil temperature.

* Air Pollution - Air pollution affects tundra environments in different ways chemicals used in coolants and aerosol sprays have driven ozone depletion at north & south poles. This can let in stronger ultraviolet rays.

* Industrial activity - The oil, gas and mining industries can disrupt fragile tundra habitats. Heavy vehicles and pipeline construction can damage soil. This also increases the rise of toxic spills.

Improvement steps

Steps to improve quality of soil, air & water.

For soil quality :

  • * Avoid repeatedly walking over the same area when the active layer of soils is thawed.
  • * Avoid driving vehicles on heavy equipment over the same area.
  • * Avoid scraping dry soil.

For air quality:

  • * Conserve energy
  • * Look for “ energy stars “ label before buying on equipment.
  • * keep engines properly tuned.
  • * Avoid burning leaves & Cutting trees.

For water :

  • * Drain your water heater annually.
  • * Ensure proper drainage.
  • * Construct a Rain garden.
  • * Use of water filtration systems.
